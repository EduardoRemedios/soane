## Version
v1

## Change Log
- v1 (2026-07-12): Stage E pre-mortem.

## Failure Scenarios

### PM-001 - Vocabulary changes without semantic consistency
Documents use Claim, fact, understanding, and truth differently. Mitigation: one definition chain and cross-document term scan.

### PM-002 - Soane absorbs external authority
Project Memory or delegation wording implies authority creation. Mitigation: preserve source-system ownership and Aegis authority references explicitly.

### PM-003 - Privacy becomes aspirational
Memory rights are listed without fail-closed propagation or promotion rules. Mitigation: require scope and restrictions to follow derived knowledge.

### PM-004 - Markdown round trips destroy authored meaning
Generated content overwrites constitutional text. Mitigation: three explicit modes and reviewed write-back only.

### PM-005 - New concepts appear implemented
Canonical docs imply a runtime Claim or Decision Review registry. Mitigation: state doctrine-now/runtime-later boundary in architecture, state, and roadmap.

### PM-006 - Metrics reward process volume
Teams optimize object counts. Mitigation: prohibit artifact count as a success proxy and measure outcomes, reliability, and attention.

## Highest-Risk Moment

The highest risk is changing “source of truth” without preserving source authority. Every relevant amendment must distinguish Workspace understanding from the records and governance owned elsewhere.
